Sensor Activity (GRADED)

You will be graded on your successful completion of some of the activities below (both easy activities, 2 of 3 moderate activities, and 1 of 2 hard activities). Starter code is provided; however, it is necessary to modify the code to complete the task. Use your experience with Python programming concepts (loops, conditionals, order of operations, functions, etc.) and the previous exploration examples to complete each task.

Once complete with one of the easy options, verify it with your instructor. This is part of your grade! Move on to the next section (Robotics), and return to complete the 4 remaining activities (the other easy activity, 2 of 3 moderate activities, and 1 of 2 hard activities) after you complete the Robotics section.

Easy Activities
  1. Transmit a message in Morse Code using the NeoPixels or Speaker. Morse Code Details
  2. Create a nightlight. If the light level gets below a certain level (recommend 20), turn the NeoPixels on in whatever color you want. Once the light is above another level (should be three or four levels greater than the turn on level), turn the NeoPixels off. Make sure you have a delay of at least 1 second between each iteration.
Moderate Activities
  1. If the switch is on, the code should do step A. If the switch is off, the code should do step B.
    1. If button A is pushed, set all the NeoPixels on that side of the board to red and the other side to blue. If button B is pushed, set the the NeoPixels on that side of the board to red and the other side to blue. If neither button is pushed, turn off the NeoPixels.
    2. Have the NeoPixels flash red, white, and blue for ½ second each.
  2. Using the A and B buttons steer your turtle around the screen. A should turn the turtle right and B should turn the turtle left. Also, if the switch is on, the turtle should move forward. If the switch is off, the turtle shouldn't move.
  3. Use the accelerometer and the NeoPixels to indicate which way the board is tilted.
Hard Activities
  1. Play a short song using the built-in speaker and the notes to frequency information here: Frequency Chart . Note: You probably want to work in Octaves 4 or 5
  2. Display three different images on your LCD. Each image should display for three seconds before going to the next image. You can choose your own images (though you'll need to scale them to 240x240 pixels and save them as bitmaps. You can do this with the paint program.) or you can use the three images provided in the Circuit Playground Lab Files folder.

Verify the completion of one easy activity with your instructor. This is part of your grade! Move on to the next section (Robotics), and return to complete the 4 remaining activities (the other easy activity, 2 of 3 moderate activities, and 1 of 2 hard activities) after you complete the Robotics section.

When complete with ALL required sensor activities AND the robotics section, complete the DAAW Honor Acknowledgement Statement in Canvas.
